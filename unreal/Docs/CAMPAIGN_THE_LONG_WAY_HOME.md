# THE LONG WAY HOME — Campaign Design (v2)

> Supersedes the structure of "Operation Plastic Storm" (CAMPAIGN_MISSIONS.md). The
> mission MECHANICS designed there (eliminate / secure / defend / sabotage / stealth /
> rescue / extract / boss) are kept — they're re-mapped onto a journey.

## The premise

A squad of green toy soldiers is flying their toy plane home to the toy store when
they're shot down — they **crash through a window into a stranger's house**. The house
(and the world beyond it) is held by the tan army. The whole campaign is one long
fighting retreat **home**: escape the house, cross the street, survive the detour, and
reach the toy store.

The tone: Army Men — big furniture, toy-scale drama, played straight.

## The structural rule (per Sean)

**No wave-defense filler.** Missions are built around **exploration**: you move through
a real space room-to-room / zone-to-zone, and you ENCOUNTER the enemy — outposts dug in
at furniture, patrols crossing lanes, garrisons at the chokepoints. Fights happen where
the enemy lives, not where a spawner says so. Every mission ends by REACHING somewhere
(a door, a gate, a vehicle), not by surviving a timer.

## The journey (each environment = one mission)

| # | Mission | Environment | Core verbs (from original plan) | Story beat |
|---|---------|-------------|--------------------------------|------------|
| 1 | **Crash Site** | Living room | Explore, clear outposts, BREACH the door | The crash. Squad regroups at the wreck, fights to the hallway door. |
| 2 | **The Hallway** | Hallway + stairs landing | Explore, stealth optional (sleeping cat), defend the doormat while the front door is forced | First sight of "outside" under the front door. |
| 3 | **The Kitchen** | Kitchen floor + counters | Sabotage (tip the kettle / cut the power), vertical fights up chair legs | Tan brass taunts them over the house radio. |
| 4 | **Out the Door** | Front porch + garden path | Escort + extract under fire | **THE LOSS: a squadmate is taken/downed covering the exit.** The squad can't go straight — they swear the detour. |
| 5 | **The Street** | Sidewalk, gutter, a storm drain | Recon/stealth at night, rescue intel on where their friend was taken | Grief turns to purpose. |
| 6 | **The Backyard War** | Neighbor's backyard | Combined arms assault on the tan staging ground; **rescue the lost squadmate** | The reunion — or the goodbye, if players failed objectives along the way. |
| 7 | **The Toy Store** | Toy store aisles | Boss + final extract to the shelf they call home | Home. |

## What carries between missions

- The same 4 soldiers (Leader / Heavy / Sniper / Medic) — names, accents, and (later)
  persistent state. The loss in M4 is scripted to the story, not to attrition.
- The wrecked plane is the campaign's visual motif: it appears in M1 (fresh crash), and
  a toy plane on a shelf is the final shot of M7.

## Mission 1 — CRASH SITE (current build target)

- Squad spawns AT the crashed plane (green toy plane wreck, nose into the floor).
- The living room is held by tan: **outpost at the coffee table**, **outpost at the
  ottoman**, **position at the book stack**, **garrison at the hallway door**, plus
  1–2 **roaming units** crossing the lanes (first contact happens on the move).
- Objective: *fight through to the hallway door and breach it* — reach the glowing
  exit zone and hold it briefly (uncontested) to win. Killing everyone is optional;
  clearing the door garrison is, practically, not.
- No counterattack waves. The enemy you meet is the enemy that lives there.
